The Goal

My 2021 New Year's resolution is to read every night before bed, even if just a few pages.

The goal of this is to:

  1. Get me to read more books this year
  2. Help me to get better sleep by not using screens directly before sleeping
  3. And most importantly, to build a lasting habit of reading before bed

The Code Book by Simon Singh
    

The Code Book is a brief history of cryptography, with some explanation of the mathematics of cryptography. It begins with descriptions of simple ciphers, and ends with speculation on future cryptography in a world with quantum computing.

The Code Book is my favorite book of the year so far. The history of cryptography is extremely interesting, and it brings up lots of important moments in history that hinged on whether a specific cipher could be broken. These important historical events give The Code Book a surprising amount of suspense. The descriptions of the math behind cryptography are very good, and do a great job of providing intuition and pros/cons without getting lost in the weeds.

Some interesting takeaways:

  • A cryptographic system is only as strong as its weakest link. No matter how mathematically sound it is, if used incorrectly
  • Cryptography is a thankless job. Many great cryptographers never recieved recognition for their work, or it was only declassified decades later
